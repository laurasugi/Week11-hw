California Redwoods

The Redwoods of California

Explore the amazing redwoods of northern California! From rugged coastal terrain to woodlands riverlands, the California redwoods have something for every level of activity and outdoor enthuiasm. Also, if you're more of a nerd than an outdoorsman these redwoods have been the setting for many amazing movies!

MUIR NATIONAL PARK

Muir Woods National Monument

Located just outside San Francisco, Muir woods is located in Marin County, California. The 554 acre park includes Mt. Tamalpias and one of the only remaining old-growth redwood forrests in the area. You may recognize the woods as the setting for Rise of the Planet of the Apes, however that movie was actually filmed in British Colombia.

WELCOME TO ENDOR

Redwood National and State Parks

Redwood national park in Humbolt county is an incredibly beautiful and surreal old growth rainforest. There are hikes ranging from easy strolls to weeklong backpacking trips. There is also a huge range of terrain, with the park including coastlands, mountains and rivers. The Smith and Klamath rivers both run through the park, and the park also includes large prarie areas. Perhaps one of my favorite things about the park is that the Tall Tree grove was used for shooting most of the Endor scenes in Star Wars: The Return of the Jedi!
